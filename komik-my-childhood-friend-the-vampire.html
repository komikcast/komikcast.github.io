35. Read the story about my uncle's childhood!Change the words in the brackets into good forms!


Jawaban:

One day, my uncle told me about his childhood. When he was ten years old, he liked to climb a mango tree. He usually climbed the tree with some friends. Then, they picked some mangoes without permission. Yup, they liked to steal mangoes. They frequently ate mangoes on the tree. They had prepared a fruit peeler and a plastic bag for the garbage. What a good preparation! They did not want to throw the garbage everywhere. They kept the environment clean. That's a good point, but stealing fruit is a bad point.

My uncle said it was fun. Sometimes, they were caught by the owners. They had ever met both kind and unkind owners. A kind owner was never angry to them. She/He let them pick some mangoes. "Don't eat too many mangoes or you will get stomachache" a kind owner advised one day. Meanwhile, an unkind owner punished them to clean a pool and backyard.

There was one owner who was really angry to them. Then, he came to the kids' parents and asked for much money for the mangoes they had eaten. Their parents couldn't afford it. The owner gave them some weeks to pay or he would call the police. Then he and his friends tried to earn money to help their parents. After school, he worked at a car wash.

My uncle learned that earning money wasn't easy, but it wasn't impossible. After three weeks, he could give the money to the mango-tree owner. Magically, the owner turned into a nice guy and asked my uncle to keep the money. He gave the money to his parents. Finally, he never stole the mangoes anymore, but sill liked to climb a mango tree.

tell menjadi toldis menjadi waswork menjadi workedcan menjadi couldsteal menjadi stole
