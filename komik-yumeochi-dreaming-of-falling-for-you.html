10. make the taq form each of the follow ing sentences A. he was rever late for school B.the snow wasn't falling too heavily


Tag question adalah pertanyaan singkat yang terdapat pada akhir kalimat. Dalam Bahasa Indonesia, tag question berarti kan?/ya?/yuk?/bukan?

Ketentuan dalam penulisan tag question antara lain:

Jika kalimatnya positif, maka tag questionnya adalah negatif

Contoh:

Wulan is smart, isn't she?

Andika likes anime, doesn't he?

You are an English teacher, aren't you?


Jika kalimatnya negatif, maka tag questionnya akan positif.

Contoh:

Wulan is not smart, is she?

Andika doesn't like anime, does he?

You are not an English teacher, are you?


Jika kalimatnya berupa larangan atau perintah, maka tag questionnya adalah 'will you?'

Contoh:

Close the door, will you?

Don't disturb me, will you?

Don't be noisy, will you?


Jika makna kalimatnya adalah negatif (mengantung kata-kata seperti no one, none, seldom, rarely, nothing, barely, dan lain-lain), maka question tagnya akan positif.

Contoh:

No one come to school, do they?

She never seems to care, does she?


Jika subjectnya adalah everything, something, nothing, maka question tag akan menggunakan subject it.

Contoh:

Something is moving, isn't it?

Everyhing will be okay, won't it?


Pembahasan

make the tag question from each of the following sentences


A. he was never late for school = He was never late for school, was he?


B.the snow wasn't falling too heavily = The snow wasn't falling too heavily, was it?

16. What is the meaning of the sentence below? a. Don’t be dreaming b. Not to be day dreaming c. You must be go on d. You must try hard to reach your life e. Don’t be weak person​


Jawaban:

The meanings of the sentences are as follows:

a. "Don’t be dreaming" - This sentence suggests not to have unrealistic or fanciful thoughts or hopes and to stay grounded in reality.

b. "Not to be daydreaming" - This sentence advises against engaging in idle or unfocused daydreaming, encouraging someone to stay attentive and focused.

c. "You must be go on" - This sentence is grammatically incorrect. The correct form would be "You must go on," which means you need to continue or persevere in your actions.

d. "You must try hard to reach your life" - This sentence is a bit unclear in meaning. It seems to imply that one should put in a significant effort to achieve their life goals.

e. "Don’t be a weak person" - This sentence advises against displaying emotional or physical weakness and encourages strength or resilience.

19. Can you help me to find exposition,rising action,climax,falling action and resolution from drama Waiting for Godot by S. Becket ?


Waiting for Godot (/ˈɡɒdoʊ/ GOD-oh[1]) is a play by Samuel Beckett, in which two characters, Vladimir and Estragon, wait for the arrival of someone named Godot who never arrives, and while waiting they engage in a variety of discussions and encounter three other characters.[2] Waiting for Godot is Beckett's translation of his own original French play, En attendant Godot, and is subtitled (in English only) "a tragicomedy in two acts".[3] The original French text was composed between 9 October 1948 and 29 January 1949.[4] The premiere was on 5 January 1953 in the Théâtre de Babylone, Paris. The English language version was premiered in London in 1955. In a poll conducted by the British Royal National Theatre in 1990 it was voted the "most significant English language play of the 20th century".[5]
