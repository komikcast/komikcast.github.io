ComparativeExact Similarity

Untuk menyatakan dua kata benda yang memiliki kesamaan dengan memasukkan kata sifat sebagai kualitasnya, gunakan rumus berikut ini.

→ Noun 1 + To be (is/am/re) + as + Adjective + as + Noun 2

Untuk menyatakan dua subject yang memiliki wujud/benda(noun) yang sama, gunakan rumus berikut ini.

→ Subject + Verb + the + same + Noun + as + Subject 2

Pembahasan

Untuk no.1 dan no.2, gunakan rumus pertama  untuk melengkapi kalimatnya.

Jawaban no.1 :  The Petronas tower 1 is as old as The Petronas tower 2.

Jawaban no.2 : The Petronas tower 1 is as high as The Petronas tower 2.

Untuk no.3 gunakan rumus kedua untuk melengkapi kalimatnya karena yang dijadikan perbandingan di kalimat no.3 adalah kata benda(noun) bukan kata sifat(adjective).

And now we are going to read the text and answer the questions provided below.

The Rain

Rain always comes from clouds. But where do clouds come from? How does all the water get into the sky?

Think about your bathroom. There is hot water in your bath. Steam goes up from the hot water. The steam makes small clouds in the bathroom. These warm clouds meet the cold walls and windows. Then we see small drops of water on the walls and windows.

The world is like your bathroom. The water in the oceans is warm when the sun shines on it. Some of this water goes up into the sky and makes clouds. The wind carries the clouds for hundreds of kilometers. The clouds meet the cold air in the sky. Then we’ll see drops of water after the condensation of clouds to water. The drops of water are rain.

The rain falls and runs into rivers. Rivers run into oceans. And the water from the oceans changes into clouds and becomes rain. So water is always moving from oceans to clouds to rain to rivers to oceans.

3. The sequenced explanation of the text is as follows:

- Rain comes from clouds.

- Clouds are formed when water evaporates from the oceans due to the sun's heat.

- The evaporated water forms clouds in the sky.

- Wind carries the clouds for long distances.

- When the clouds meet cold air in the sky, the water vapor condenses into small drops of water.

- These drops of water become rain.

- The rain falls to the ground and runs into rivers.

- Rivers flow into the oceans.

- The water from the oceans evaporates again, forming more clouds and starting the cycle anew.

- Therefore, water is always moving through the cycle from oceans to clouds to rain to rivers and back to the oceans.
