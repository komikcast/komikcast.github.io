2. List the Himalayan rivers and the peninsular rivers that helped the formation of north indian plain


Answer: 

North Indian Plain is one of the most fertile area in South Asia. This plain's location stretches from Himalayas to Peninsular Plateau. The formation of this plain is developed by three Himalayan rivers. Those are Ganges, Indus and Brahmaputra. Meanwhile the Peninsular rivers which also does the same role of those are Narmada, Manahari and Godavari. There are some differences between Himalayan dan Peninsular rivers, they are
the shape: Himalayan is meandering ; Peninsular is straight
the water source: snow & rain ; only rain
the valley: V-shaped ; U-shaped

3. The condition of rivers changes due to human activities such as pollution, deforestation, and development, as well as natural factors like drought and climate change.

5. This can have lasting effects on both the local and downstream ecosystems and can even impact human health.
