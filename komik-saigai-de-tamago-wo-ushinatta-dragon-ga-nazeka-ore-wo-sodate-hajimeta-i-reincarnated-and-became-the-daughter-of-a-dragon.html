26. The main idea dragon fly and damselfly


Dragonflies and damselflies are both types of insects that belong to the order Odonata. Although they may look similar, they have distinct differences in terms of their physical features and behaviors. Dragonflies are larger in size, have wider wings, and hold their wings perpendicular to their bodies. They are also known to be stronger and faster fliers. On the other hand, damselflies are smaller, have narrower wings, and hold their wings parallel to their bodies. They are also more delicate in terms of their strength and flying abilities. Both dragonflies and damselflies are skilled hunters that rely on their keen eyesight to catch their prey. They are also important for the ecosystem, as they help control the insect population and serve as food for other animals.
