2. "I have told you" or "I had told you"? Which is true? Explain!​


Both "I have told you" and "I had told you" can be true, but they are used in different contexts and convey different meanings.

"I have told you" is used in the present perfect tense. It implies that the action of telling you occurred in the past but has a connection or relevance to the present. This phrase suggests that the speaker has previously communicated the information to the listener and is emphasizing the current state of the situation.

Here is the analysis:

The complete sentence is:

Mayang: Why didn't you come to my house last night?

I would have visited you if I had had much time.

This is a second conditional sentence, used to express an imaginary or hypothetical condition in the past.

The structure is:

If + past perfect (had had), would have + past participle (would have visited)

So the correct option to fill in the blank is:

C. had had

This constructs the past perfect tense (had had) required in the if-clause of a second conditional sentence.

The answer is C.
