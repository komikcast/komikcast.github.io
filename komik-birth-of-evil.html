11. Short bio about cut nyak dhien Name, place of birth, date of birth, place of death,parents and origins, name of husband, name of daughter, important dates on war


Recount text is a kind of the which is used to retell a story or an event in the past. Recount text is used to inform or entertain the readers.

The generic structure of recount text

1. Orientation

2. Events

3. Re-orientation

Biography is a text which tells about someone’s life. Biography is an example of recount text.

Here is a short biography of Cut Nyak Dhien

Cut Nyak Dhien was a leader of Acehnese forces during the Aceh war. She was born into an Islamic aristocratic family in 1848 in Lampadang, Aceh. Her father is Teuku Nanta Setia was a member of the ruling Ulèë Balang aristocratic class in VI mukim and her mother was also from an aristocrat family. Cut Nyak Dhien married to Teuku Cek Ibrahim Lamnga in 1892.

On 26 March 1873, the Dutch declared war on Aceh, beginning the Aceh War. The Dutch captured some important places in Aceh successfully. On 29 June 1878, Her husband died in the war. Hearing this, Cut Nyak Dhien swore revenge against the Dutch. Some time after the death of her husband, an Acehnese hero Teuku Umar proposed to her. She accepted his proposal when Umar allowed her to fight, and they were married in 1880. Teuku Umar and Cut Nyak Dhien had a daughter together named Cut Gambang. The war continued, Teuku Umar surrendered to Dutch forces on 30 September 1893. Two years later, he betrayed the Dutch and brought weapons to help the Acehnese. Teuku Umar was killed during battle when the Dutch launched a surprise attack on him in Meulaboh.

After her husband died, Cut Nyak Dhien continued to resist the Dutch with her small army until its destruction in 1901. Her condition was getting weaker as she got older. In the end she was captured and sent into exile to Sumedang, West Java on 11 December 1905. She died on 6 November 1908.
