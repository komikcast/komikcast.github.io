For thousands of years in a substantial number of cultures and nations, women have served in various roles in the military, from ancient warrior women to those currently serving in current armed conflicts.

While the majority of combatants in most cultures have been men, a number of women in history have fought alongside them. In the American Civil War, there were women who sometimes even fought cross-dressed as men. Fighting on the battlefront was not the only way women involved themselves in war. Despite various roles in the armies of past societies, the role of women in the military, particularly in combat, is controversial and it is only recently that women have begun to be given a more prominent role in contemporary armed forces. 31. active sentence is changed to passive voice：the teacher punished his students who are not doing homework！​


His students who are not doing homework were punished by the teacher.

or

The teacher's students who are not doing homework were punished.

EXPLANATION

Passive voice is used when we want to emphasize the action and recipient of the verb.

The steps how to change the active voice to the passive voice are :

~ pay attention to the verb form whether it's transitive or not. We can't change the active voice that contains an intransitive verb.

~ the object of active voice is moved to the subject of passive voice. Meanwhile, the subject of active voice is changed into the object of passive voice accompanied with the "by" preposition or sometimes it's omitted.

~ all of the verbs are changed into the past participle verb (V³).

BASED ON YOUR QUESTION

Simple Past

A : S+Verb²+O

P : S(O)+to be(was/were)+verb³+by Agent

The teacher (Subject) punished (verb) his students who are not doing homework (Object).

changes to :

The teacher's students who are not doing homework (New subject) were punished (verb).

➡ "His students" is changed to "the teacher's students" because we already know who the agent is and we don't have to expose the agent "by the teacher." It's ambiguous when we put "his students" in the front of passive voice.
