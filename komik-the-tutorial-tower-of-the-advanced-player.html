The tower building has a large clock right at the top of the tower and is called the 'Jam Gadang' which in Minangkabau language or which in Indonesian means 'Big Clock'. Apart from being a symbol of the city, the existence of the gadang clock is also used as a tourist attraction that can attract tourists. It turns out that behind the splendor of the gadang clock, there is history in its manufacture.

According to several historical figures, the jam gadang was built in 1926 during the reign of the Dutch East Indies as a gift from the Queen of the Netherlands to Rook Maker, a secretary from the city of Bukittingi. The design of this clock gadang was designed by an architect named Yazid Rajo Mangkuto. Its construction is estimated to cost up to 3,000 Guilders which is a very large expense at that time.

The Clock Tower was made by several workers and took quite a long time. In addition to the size of the tower which is high and large, the length of time and costs incurred have also become the center of attention of many people so that the Jam Gadang tower is starting to be widely known by people.

The person who laid the first stone when the construction of the Jam Gadang tower was completed was the Rook Maker who was 6 years old at that time and was a symbol that the Clock Tower had been officially given to the city of Bukittinggi and served as a marker and also the zero point of the city.

Since its establishment until now, the clock tower has been changed several times, especially in the shape of the roof. When the tower was first built, during the Dutch East Indies era, the roof of the tower was round with a statue of a rooster on it facing east.

Then when Indonesia was colonized by Japan, the roof of the tower was changed into a shape like a pagoda, finally after Indonesia became independent, the tower was then converted into a shape like the custom of a Minangkabau house to become a symbol of the Minangkabau tribe.

In addition, the Clock Tower has also been renovated by the Bukittinggi city government in collaboration with the Indonesian Heritage Preservation Agency (BPPI) and the Dutch-Indonesian embassy. Until now, the Clock Tower tower continues to be maintained and preserved its existence.
