2. how to open a bank account teks procedure ​


Jawaban:

Opening a bank account typically involves a series of steps and requirements. Here's a general procedure to open a bank account:

1. Research and choose a bank: Start by researching different banks in your area or online. Consider factors like the bank's reputation, services offered, fees, and convenience.

2. Gather necessary documents: Banks require certain documents to open an account. Generally, you'll need the following:

- Proof of identity: Valid government-issued identification such as a passport, driver's license, or national ID card.

- Proof of address: Documents like utility bills, rental agreements, or bank statements that confirm your residential address.

- Social Security Number (SSN) or Tax Identification Number (TIN).

- Employment details: Information about your employer, job title, and income source.

3. Visit the bank: Once you've chosen a bank, visit the nearest branch in person. Schedule an appointment if required, especially during busy times.

4. Speak to a bank representative: Inform the bank representative that you want to open a bank account. They will guide you through the process and provide you with the necessary forms to fill out.

5. Fill out the application form: Complete the application form provided by the bank. The form will require personal details such as your name, address, date of birth, occupation, and contact information.

6. Submit the required documents: Present your identification documents, proof of address, and any other required documents to the bank representative. They will verify the information and make copies for their records.

7. Choose an account type: Banks offer various types of accounts, such as savings accounts, current accounts, or specialized accounts. Discuss your requirements and select the type of account that best suits your needs.

8. Deposit initial funds: Some banks require an initial deposit to activate the account. Determine the minimum deposit amount and provide the funds to the bank representative. They will credit it to your new account.

9. Review account terms and sign agreements: Carefully read any account agreements, terms, and conditions provided by the bank. Seek clarification if you have any questions. Once satisfied, sign the necessary documents to finalize the account opening process.

10. Receive account details: After completing the paperwork, the bank will provide you with your account details, including the account number, debit card, and any other relevant information.

11. Set up online banking (optional): If the bank offers online banking services, inquire about the procedure to set up online access. This will allow you to manage your account online and perform various banking transactions.

12. Berikan materi tentang south-south and triangular cooperation (sstc) to strengthen economy in indonesia


Jawaban:

South-South Cooperation (SSC) adalah kerja sama yang dilakukan oleh negara-negara di kawasan dunia yang berada di belahan selatan (Southern Hemisphere) untuk saling membantu dalam pembangunan. SSC ini difokuskan pada pemberian bantuan teknis, pengalaman, keahlian, dan pengetahuan yang bermanfaat bagi pengembangan ekonomi dan kesejahteraan masyarakat di negara-negara yang bersangkutan.

Triangular Cooperation (TC) adalah kerja sama yang dilakukan oleh tiga pihak, yaitu negara-negara maju, negara-negara berkembang, dan lembaga donor atau organisasi internasional. TC ini bertujuan untuk meningkatkan kapasitas pembangunan di negara-negara berkembang dengan memanfaatkan keunggulan dan kekuatan masing-masing pihak yang terlibat.

South-South and Triangular Cooperation (SSTC) merupakan kerja sama yang dilakukan oleh negara-negara di belahan selatan dunia dan mencakup lembaga donor atau organisasi internasional. SSTC ini diharapkan dapat memperkuat ekonomi Indonesia dengan beberapa cara, yakni:

1. Meningkatkan akses terhadap sumber daya teknologi dan keahlian yang dibutuhkan untuk pembangunan ekonomi.

2. Meningkatkan kapasitas sumber daya manusia di Indonesia dengan bantuan pelatihan dan pendidikan dari negara-negara lain.

3. Meningkatkan diversifikasi produk dan pasar ekspor Indonesia melalui kerja sama dengan negara-negara lain.

4. Menciptakan peluang investasi di Indonesia dengan menarik investor dari negara-negara di belahan selatan dunia.

5. Meningkatkan kemitraan bisnis dan pertukaran teknologi dengan negara-negara lain untuk meningkatkan inovasi dan produktivitas di Indonesia.

6. Mendorong kerja sama yang lebih erat antara negara-negara di belahan selatan dunia untuk meningkatkan kemandirian ekonomi dan pembangunan di Indonesia.

21. How to prevent open burning


bagaimana mencegah pembakaran terbuka. itu artinya, semoga membantu ^_^

1

What is the purpose of this guide?

The purpose of this guide is to describe the problems of open burning and explain the provisions for its control under the Air Pollution Control (Open Burning) Regulation. Copies of the Regulation are on sale at the Publications Sales Section of the Information Services Department (ISD) and the online Government Bookstore at http://www.gov.hk/en/apps/isdbookstore.htm. Also, they can be found on the website of the Bilingual Laws Information at http://www.legislation.gov.hk. Enquiries concerning the Regulation may be made to the Regional Offices and their addresses are given in the Annex.

2 What is Open Burning?

Open burning means burning of any materials in the open air without any enclosure nor any chimney to direct the smoke away so generated. For example, recovering metal from used electric wires or tyres, and disposal of wastes in construction sites and countryside by open burning are the common malpractice.

3 How does it affect us?

Open burning of wastes generates excessive emissions of pollutants such as dense and odorous smoke, dust, and toxic fumes. These emissions often cause serious nuisances and may threaten the health of the people in the neighbourhood.

4 How do we deal with the problem?

The Air Pollution Control (Open Burning) Regulation is introduced to prohibit open burning of construction waste, tyres, and open burning for the salvage of metal. Under very special circumstances where open burning is absolutely necessary to be carried out, the burning operation will be subjected to the control of a permit. Some open burning activities not causing any substantial emissions are exempted from the control of the Regulation.

5 May I still barbecue in designated barbecue sites?

Yes, you may still enjoy barbecue. The following open burning activities are exempted from the control of the Regulation:

burning of incense, candle sticks, joss paper for ritual purposes

fires for cooking of food such as barbecue for amenity purposes

fires set for fire testing, training and education by any Government fire fighting force or agency

agricultural burning of materials grown on site, solely for weeding, land disinfection, pest control, or burning to make firebreaks in the countryside

open burning required for emergency situations to ensure public safety

6 What types of open burning activities are prohibited?

Open burning activities for the following purposes are prohibited:

the disposal of construction waste

the clearance of a site in preparation for construction work

the disposal of tyres

the salvage of metal

7 Under what circumstances can I obtain a permit?

The purpose of the Regulation is to stop all open burning. Therefore, a permit will be granted only if the Authority is fully satisfied that there is no viable alternative to achieve the purpose. If such a case arises, you may obtain the application form and submit it to any of our Regional Offices as listed in the Annex. A fee of $2,250 will be charged for each application.

8 What are the penalties?

You will be liable to a maximum fine of $50,000 on first conviction, plus $500 for every 15 minutes for which the offence continues. For subsequent conviction, you will be liable to a maximum fine of $50,000 and 3-month imprisonment, plus $500 for every 15 minutes for which the offence continues.

9 How can I help protect the environment?

As a member of society, you can help protect the environment by refraining from carrying out prohibited open burning activities, and to advise your staff and others to abide by the regulation. Any waste should be properly disposed of at landfills or recycled at a recycling plant with emission control equipment. If you notice any open burning activities, please report the incident to our Regional Offices (see Annex) immediately so that our enforcement staff can take necessary legal action under the Regulation to stop further burning

b. opening = Continues Tense
