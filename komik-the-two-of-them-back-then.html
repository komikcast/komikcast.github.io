8. 3) What are the purposes of the back-channeling used in the dialogue? Back-channeling Purposes​mohon bantuannya ya


1) Showing active listening: Back-channeling signals to the speaker that the listener is actively engaged and paying attention to what is being said. It includes responses such as "uh-huh," "yeah," "I see," or nodding of the head. These cues encourage the speaker to continue and feel that their message is being received.

2) Providing feedback: Back-channeling allows the listener to provide feedback to the speaker, indicating their understanding or agreement. For example, saying "I understand" or "That makes sense" reassures the speaker that their message is clear and well-received.

3) Encouraging the speaker: Back-channeling cues can serve as encouragement for the speaker to continue expressing their thoughts and ideas. It shows that the listener is interested and supportive, which can boost the speaker's confidence and motivation to continue speaking.

4) Managing conversational flow: Back-channeling helps to maintain a smooth flow of conversation by indicating to the speaker that the listener is following along. It helps to avoid interruptions or misunderstandings and allows for a more interactive and cooperative dialogue.

Overall, the purposes of back-channeling in a dialogue are to demonstrate active listening, provide feedback, encourage the speaker, and facilitate effective communication between the participants.
